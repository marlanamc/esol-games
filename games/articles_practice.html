Article Rules

Use "A":
  • Before singular countable nouns starting with consonant sounds
  • When mentioning something for the first time
  • Examples: a book, a university (sounds like "you"), a house
Use "An":
  • Before singular countable nouns starting with vowel sounds
  • Remember: it's about the sound, not the letter
  • Examples: an apple, an hour (silent h), an MBA
Use "The":
  • When referring to something specific or already mentioned
  • With superlatives (the best, the most)
  • With unique things (the sun, the moon)
  • With certain proper nouns (the United States)
No Article:
  • With plural countable nouns in general statements
  • With uncountable nouns in general
  • With proper nouns (names, cities, most countries)
  • Examples: Dogs are friendly, Water is essential, John lives in Paris
